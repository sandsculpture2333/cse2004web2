Scene Trap

Assemble your scenario. Step into the scene.

What is Scene Trap?

Scene Trap is a two-player improv dialogue game. You and another person play two characters trapped in a dramatic situation. The AI sets the scene — you act it out by taking turns writing what your character says.

There are no right answers. The goal is to stay in character, follow the rules, and push the scene forward.

Before you start

Sit with another person. One of you is Player A, the other is Player B. You'll share the screen and take turns typing — like passing a script back and forth.

Step 1 — Build your scene

Use the three dropdowns to pick a World (the setting), a Relationship (who these two characters are to each other), and a Scene type (what kind of situation they're in). Then click Enter the Scene.

The AI will generate:

  • Scene setup — the backstory and why these two must face each other right now
  • Shared goal — a specific thing that must happen before the scene can end (e.g. "one of them must apologise")
  • Expression constraint — a rule about how you must speak (e.g. "every line must start with 'I never'")

Read all three carefully before you begin.

Step 2 — Play the scene

Select who you are using the Player A / Player B toggle. Type what your character says and click Speak (or press Enter). Then the other player does the same.

A few rules:

  • Stay in character — speak as the character, not as yourself
  • Follow the expression constraint at all times
  • You can say anything your character would plausibly say — argue, deflect, confess, accuse
  • There is no fixed number of turns. Keep going until the shared goal has been reached
Step 3 — Use the tools
  • Trigger Chaos — inject a surprise event that shifts the balance. Use it when the scene feels stuck or too comfortable. Both players must incorporate it.
  • Claim Completion — press this when you think the shared goal has been achieved. Both players must agree for the scene to end. If only one claims it, the scene continues.
  • Report Violation — if a player broke the expression constraint or spoke out of character, flag it. You can retract their last line as a penalty.
  • Use Voice — speak your line into the microphone instead of typing. The transcript will fill in automatically.
How does it end?

The scene ends when both players click Claim Completion and both agree. This should only happen once the shared goal has genuinely been triggered through dialogue — not just mentioned or wished for, but actually played out.

Tips for first-timers
  • Read the scene setup out loud together before you begin — it helps both players get into character
  • The constraint is a challenge, not a suggestion. Breaking it on purpose is cheating
  • Don't rush to end the scene. The best moments happen when you stay in the tension longer
  • Chaos events are meant to be disruptive — don't ignore them, react to them
